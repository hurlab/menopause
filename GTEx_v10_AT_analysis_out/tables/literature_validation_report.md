## 1. Signature Genes with Strong Literature Support

### 1.1 Core Estrogen-Related Genes

#### **HSD17B2 (17β-Hydroxysteroid Dehydrogenase Type 2)**
- **Function:** Catalyzes the conversion of estradiol (E2) to estrone (E1) - the last step in estrogen biosynthesis
- **Relevance to Menopause:** Critical for controlling local estrogen levels within adipose tissue, particularly important in postmenopausal women where adipose tissue becomes the primary site of estrogen production
- **Key Literature:**
  - Roles of Matrix Metalloproteinases and Their Natural Inhibitors (2023)
  - Menopausal Hormone Therapy: Its Role in the Prevention and Treatment (2024)

#### **RXFP1 (Relaxin Family Peptide Receptor 1)**
- **Function:** Receptor for relaxin peptide hormone
- **Relevance:** Regulated by estrogen; part of the hormone signaling network that changes during menopausal transition
- **Key Pathway:** Estrogen receptor signaling pathway

---

### 1.2 Adipose Tissue and Adipogenesis Genes

#### **NNAT (Neuronatin)**
- **Function:** Highly enriched in white adipose tissue
- **Key Findings:**
  - Regulates **adipocyte differentiation**, metabolism, and thermogenesis
  - Positively associated with **BMI** and **body fat percentage**
  - Plays crucial role in adipose tissue function and metabolic regulation
- **Source:** Human Protein Atlas, adipose tissue expression studies

#### **CEMIP (Cell Migration-Inducing Protein, formerly KIAA1199)**
- **Function:** Regulates adipogenesis through WNT-β-catenin signaling
- **Key Findings:**
  - **2025 study:** Demonstrated importance in adipose tissue biology
  - Regulates whole-body energy metabolism
  - Critical convergence point for Wnt signaling and adipogenesis
- **Pathway:** Wnt/β-catenin signaling pathway

#### **ACAN (Aggrecan)**
- **Function:** Extracellular matrix proteoglycan
- **Relevance:** ECM remodeling in adipose tissue during obesity and metabolic changes
- **Context:** Adipose tissue expansion requires ECM remodeling

---

### 1.3 Wnt Signaling Pathway Genes

#### **SFRP2 (Secreted Frizzled-Related Protein 2)**
- **Function:** Wnt signaling inhibitor/modulator
- **Key Findings:**
  - Associated with increased **adiposity** and **VEGF expression**
  - **Down-regulates estrogen-dependent β-catenin activity**
  - Wnt pathway serves as critical intersection between estrogen signaling and adipogenesis
  - Inhibition of Wnt signaling promotes preadipocyte differentiation
- **Biological Significance:** Connects estrogen status to adipose tissue remodeling

---

### 1.4 Extracellular Matrix Remodeling Genes

#### **MMP3 (Matrix Metalloproteinase-3, Stromelysin-1)**
- **Function:** ECM remodeling protease
- **Key Findings:**
  - **Anti-adipogenic factor** - inhibits adipocyte differentiation
  - Modulates ECM remodeling required for fat depot expansion
  - **Serum MMP3 levels correlate with postmenopausal status** and bone mineral density
  - **Estrogen influences MMP3 expression**
  - High-fat diet-induced obesity regulates MMP3 expression
  - Works with TIMPs to control adipose tissue inflammation and insulin sensitivity
- **Relevance to Menopause:** Biomarker for inflammatory and metabolic changes during menopause

#### **COL11A1 (Collagen Type XI Alpha 1)**
- **Function:** ECM structural component
- **Relevance:** Mammary collagen is under reproductive control; changes with hormonal status
- **Context:** ECM remodeling crucial for adipose tissue expansion

#### **KRT17, KRT19 (Keratins)**
- **Function:** Intermediate filament proteins
- **Relevance:**
  - KRT17 identified as biomarker in various contexts
  - Keratins involved in stromal cell function
  - Single-cell RNA-seq reveals unique landscape in breast/adipose tissue
- **Context:** Stromal remodeling during menopausal transition

---

### 1.5 Metabolic and Hormone-Related Genes

#### **SCG2 (Secretogranin II)**
- **Function:** Member of secretogranin/chromogranin family involved in neuropeptide and hormone functions
- **Key Findings:**
  - **Estrogen-dependent expression** confirmed
  - Involved in peptide hormone sorting
  - Related to ovarian folliculogenesis
  - Changes in secretory granule proteins observed in menopause-related studies
- **Relevance:** Connects neuroendocrine function with menopausal transition

#### **PCSK9 (Proprotein Convertase Subtilisin/Kexin Type 9)**
- **Function:** Regulates LDL cholesterol metabolism
- **Context:** Lipid metabolism changes significantly during menopausal transition
- **Relevance:** Part of metabolic syndrome risk profile that increases after menopause

#### **GALNT5 (Polypeptide N-Acetylgalactosaminyltransferase 5)**
- **Function:** O-linked glycosylation enzyme
- **Relevance:**
  - Related to GALNT2 which regulates insulin sensitivity, lipid metabolism, and fat homeostasis
  - Glycosylation affects hormone receptor function
  - Novel role for GalNAc-T dependent glycosylation in energy homeostasis (2022)

#### **MUCL1 (Mucin-Like 1)**
- **Function:** Small Breast Epithelial Mucin (SBEM)
- **Key Findings:**
  - Estrogen influences mucinous breast cancer subtypes
  - **MUC1 expression significantly decreased in postmenopausal females**
  - Postmenopausal estrone and obesity drive ER+ breast cancer progression
  - Adipose stromal cells enhance ER+ breast cancer proliferation via leptin
- **Relevance:** Biomarker for menopausal status and hormone-related cancer risk

---

### 1.6 Stem Cell and Niche Regulation

#### **CXCL12/CXCR4 Signaling Axis** (Pathway relevance)
- **Function:** Stem cell niche maintenance
- **Key Findings:**
  - **Estrogen directly induces SDF-1/CXCL12 gene expression**
  - **SERMs repress CXCL12 expression**
  - Breast adipose tissue is crucial for estrogen production in postmenopausal women (35% higher estrogen, 130% higher estradiol in obese postmenopausal women)
  - Adiponectin and leptin from adipose tissue modulate estrogen production
- **Relevance:** Estrogen regulation of stem cell niche in adipose tissue microenvironment
